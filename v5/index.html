Happy School Vacation in Yunnan!

The ultimate family adventure guide to one of China's most amazing provinces

1 Kunming and the Stone Forest

Welcome to Kunming, the "Spring City!" This is where your Yunnan adventure begins. Kunming has perfect weather almost all year round -- not too hot, not too cold, just right. It is like the Goldilocks of cities!

But the real star of the show is the Stone Forest, about an hour's drive from the city. Imagine a giant forest, but instead of trees, everything is made of tall, pointy rocks! Some of them look like animals, some look like people, and some look like castles from a fairy tale. You can walk through narrow paths between towering stone pillars and feel like you have shrunk down to the size of an ant.

The local Sani people have lived near the Stone Forest for centuries, and they have wonderful stories about how the rocks got there. Ask a local guide to tell you the legend of Ashima -- it is one of the most famous love stories in all of Yunnan!

Did You Know?

These rocks are 270 million years old! That means they were here before the dinosaurs even existed. The Stone Forest used to be an ancient sea floor, and over millions of years, the water carved the limestone into these incredible shapes.

Adventure Checklist

  • Walk through the Major Stone Forest and find a rock that looks like an animal
  • Visit the Minor Stone Forest for a quieter, more magical experience
  • Try Kunming's famous "Crossing the Bridge" rice noodles for lunch
  • Watch a Sani cultural dance performance

2 Dali

Dali is one of those places that makes you say "Wow!" the moment you arrive. Picture this: a sparkling blue lake stretching as far as you can see, with snow-capped mountains rising behind it. That lake is called Erhai, and those mountains are the famous Cangshan range. Together, they make one of the most beautiful views in all of China.

The old town of Dali is surrounded by ancient walls and gates that are hundreds of years old. Inside, the streets are paved with stone and lined with shops selling tie-dye cloth, handmade jewelry, and all sorts of treats. The Bai people who live here are famous for being super friendly and for making incredible food.

One of the coolest things to see is the Three Pagodas of Chongsheng Temple. These three tall towers have been standing for over a thousand years, and the reflection of the pagodas in the water is one of the most photographed scenes in all of Yunnan.

Rent bikes and ride along the shore of Erhai Lake with your family. The path goes through tiny villages where farmers grow vegetables and fishermen cast their nets. Stop for a snack at one of the lakeside cafes and watch the clouds drift over the mountains. It is pure magic.

Did You Know?

Erhai Lake is so big you can see it from space! It covers about 250 square kilometers, which is roughly the size of 35,000 soccer fields. The name "Erhai" means "Ear Sea" because the lake is shaped like a human ear when you look at it from above.

Adventure Checklist

  • Ride a bike along the Erhai Lake shoreline
  • Take a photo of the Three Pagodas reflected in the water
  • Try Bai-style tie-dyeing at a workshop in the old town
  • Taste the famous Dali "rushan" cheese snack

3 Lijiang

Get ready to step back in time! Lijiang Old Town is like walking into a living history book. The wooden buildings, stone bridges, and winding canals have been here for hundreds and hundreds of years, and the whole place is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. That means the entire world agrees it is super special and worth protecting.

The Naxi people built this town, and they have their own unique writing system called Dongba script. It uses tiny pictures to represent words, kind of like ancient Egyptian hieroglyphics but way more fun to look at. You can find Dongba script carved into signs and painted on walls all over town.

And then there is the Jade Dragon Snow Mountain looming in the background. At over 5,500 meters tall, its snowy peaks shine in the sunlight like a dragon made of ice and crystal. You can take a cable car up to a viewing platform and stand in the clouds!

From the viewing platform, you can see glaciers, snow fields, and valleys spread out below you. Bring a warm jacket -- it gets cold up there even in summer! The mountain is sacred to the Naxi people and features in many of their legends.

Did You Know?

Lijiang Old Town is so old it has been here for 800 years! It was founded during the Song Dynasty and has survived earthquakes, wars, and centuries of change. Unlike most ancient Chinese cities, Lijiang was never built with a city wall because the ruling Mu family thought walls would make the character for "mu" look like the character for "trapped."

Adventure Checklist

  • Learn to write your name in Dongba script
  • Ride the cable car up Jade Dragon Snow Mountain
  • Walk along the canals in the old town at sunset
  • Listen to a traditional Naxi orchestra performance

4 Tiger Leaping Gorge

Hold on to your hats, adventurers! Tiger Leaping Gorge is one of the most jaw-dropping places on the entire planet. Imagine standing on the edge of a canyon so deep that the river at the bottom looks like a tiny blue ribbon. The walls of the gorge rise up almost 4,000 meters on either side -- that is taller than ten Empire State Buildings stacked on top of each other!

The gorge gets its name from a legend about a tiger that was being chased by a hunter. The tiger came to the narrowest part of the gorge, where a big rock sits in the middle of the raging river, and leaped right across! The river here is the mighty Jinsha (Golden Sand) River, which is actually the upper stretch of the famous Yangtze River.

Families with older kids can hike parts of the trail along the gorge rim. The views are absolutely incredible -- you will feel like you are on top of the world. For younger adventurers, the viewpoints at the road level are just as spectacular and much easier to reach.

Did You Know?

Tiger Leaping Gorge is one of the deepest canyons in the entire world! At its deepest point, it plunges about 3,790 meters from the mountain peaks to the river below. That is deeper than the Grand Canyon in the United States! The water rushes through so fast that you can hear it roaring from way up on the trail.

Adventure Checklist

  • Stand at a viewpoint and look straight down into the gorge
  • Spot the "tiger leaping rock" in the middle of the river
  • Take a family photo with the snow mountains in the background

5 Shangri-La

Welcome to Shangri-La, a place so magical that it is literally named after a fantasy world! High up on the Tibetan Plateau at over 3,000 meters above sea level, Shangri-La feels like stepping into another universe. The air is crisp and clean, the sky is the deepest blue you have ever seen, and enormous monasteries sit on hilltops like castles from a storybook.

The biggest and most impressive monastery is Songzanlin, sometimes called the "Little Potala Palace." It is home to hundreds of Tibetan Buddhist monks and is filled with colorful murals, golden statues, and the smell of butter tea and incense. Walking through its halls feels like exploring a treasure-filled palace.

The old town of Shangri-La, called Dukezong, has one of the world's biggest prayer wheels. It is so large that it takes several people pushing together to make it spin! Give it a try with your family -- spinning the prayer wheel is said to bring good luck and happiness.

Did You Know?

Shangri-La is named after a magical place in a famous book! In 1933, author James Hilton wrote a novel called "Lost Horizon" about a mysterious, perfect paradise hidden in the mountains called Shangri-La. The city was officially renamed from Zhongdian to Shangri-La in 2001 because people thought this beautiful place matched the description in the book.

Adventure Checklist

  • Help spin the giant prayer wheel in Dukezong Old Town
  • Visit Songzanlin Monastery and count the golden rooftops
  • Try yak butter tea (it tastes different from regular tea!)
  • Walk around Napa Lake and look for black-necked cranes

6 Yuanyang Rice Terraces

Prepare to have your mind blown! The Yuanyang Rice Terraces look like a giant staircase carved into the mountains by a magical giant. Thousands and thousands of curved, shimmering steps flow down the hillsides, filled with water that reflects the sky like millions of tiny mirrors. It is one of the most beautiful things humans have ever created.

The Hani people built these terraces by hand, carving each step into the steep mountain slopes and building clever water channels to keep every terrace perfectly watered. The water flows down from the misty forests at the top of the mountains, through the terraces, and into the rivers below. It is an incredible system that works perfectly with nature.

The best time to visit is during the winter and early spring, when the terraces are flooded with water and the reflections are absolutely stunning. At sunrise and sunset, the water turns gold, orange, pink, and purple. It is like watching the sky paint itself across the mountainside!

Did You Know?

Farmers have been building these terraces for 1,300 years! That means construction started way back in the Tang Dynasty, and the Hani people have been maintaining and expanding them ever since. The terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and they cover over 16,000 hectares -- that is about the size of 22,000 soccer fields!

Adventure Checklist

  • Wake up super early to watch the sunrise over the terraces
  • Visit the Duoyishu viewpoint for the most spectacular views
  • Walk along the paths between the terraces and meet local farmers
  • Try the local Hani-style red rice -- it is naturally pink!

What to Eat

Yunnan has some of the most fun and delicious food in all of China. Here are the must-try dishes for your family!

Crossing the Bridge Noodles

A big bowl of hot broth arrives at your table, and then you add all the ingredients yourself -- thin rice noodles, sliced meat, vegetables, and herbs. It is like building your own soup masterpiece! Legend says a wife invented this dish to keep food warm while crossing a bridge to bring lunch to her husband.

Erkuai (Rice Cakes)

These chewy, stretchy rice cakes can be grilled, fried, or cooked in soup. They come in all shapes -- flat rounds, thin strips, even fun shapes for kids. Grilled erkuai with sweet or savory sauce is the perfect street food snack while exploring old towns.

Rushan (Fan Cheese)

This is one of the most unique foods you will ever try! The Bai people stretch fresh cheese into thin sheets and fold them onto sticks like fans. They can be fried crispy and drizzled with rose jam or chocolate sauce. It tastes kind of like a cheesy churro!

Yunnan Mushrooms

Yunnan is the mushroom capital of China! The province has hundreds of different types of wild mushrooms. You can try mushroom hotpot, mushroom stir-fry, and even mushroom soup. Some mushrooms come in wild colors -- blue, orange, and even bright red.

Pineapple Rice

Sticky rice cooked with pineapple and served inside a hollowed-out pineapple! It is sweet, fragrant, and looks amazing. This dish comes from the Dai people in southern Yunnan and is a hit with kids and adults alike.

Rose Pastries

Kunming is famous for its roses, and the locals put them in everything -- including pastries! These flaky, buttery cakes are filled with sweet rose petal jam. They are sold everywhere and make the perfect souvenir to bring home for friends and family.

Trip Tips for Families

Everything you need to know to plan the perfect Yunnan family vacation!

When to Go

The best time for families is April to June or September to October. The weather is mild, crowds are smaller than summer peak season, and the scenery is gorgeous. Avoid the Chinese national holidays (first week of May and October) when popular spots get very crowded.

Getting Around

High-speed trains connect Kunming, Dali, and Lijiang and are super comfortable for families. For Tiger Leaping Gorge and Shangri-La, hire a private driver or join a small group tour. Yuanyang is best reached by car from Kunming (about 5-6 hours).

Altitude Awareness

Shangri-La sits at over 3,000 meters above sea level. Take it easy on your first day there -- drink lots of water, skip running around, and rest if anyone feels dizzy or headachy. Most kids adjust quickly, but it is good to be prepared.

What to Pack

  • Layers! Mountain weather changes fast
  • Comfortable walking shoes with good grip
  • Sunscreen and hats (the UV is strong at high altitude)
  • A refillable water bottle for each family member
  • A small backpack for day trips
  • Rain jacket or poncho (just in case)

Suggested Itinerary

  • Days 1-2: Kunming and Stone Forest
  • Days 3-4: Dali and Erhai Lake
  • Days 5-6: Lijiang Old Town and Jade Dragon Snow Mountain
  • Day 7: Tiger Leaping Gorge day trip
  • Days 8-9: Shangri-La
  • Days 10-11: Yuanyang Rice Terraces (or fly home from Lijiang)

Family-Friendly Reminders

  • Most restaurants can make milder dishes for kids on request
  • Bring snacks for long drives between cities
  • Download offline maps -- cell signal can be spotty in rural areas
  • Learn a few Mandarin phrases together as a family -- locals love it!
