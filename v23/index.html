YUNNAN

In-Flight Destination Guide

Seven cities. One province. Infinite altitude.

Presented by Yunnan Airways

昆明

KUNMING & THE STONE FOREST

They call Kunming the Spring City -- 15 degrees year-round, flowers in every season. But 85 kilometres southeast, the earth does something extraordinary. The Stone Forest rises from the red soil like a lithified ocean: 270-million-year-old limestone pinnacles, some towering 30 metres, carved by rain into blades and slots barely wide enough to walk through. The Sani people call it the first wonder of the world. UNESCO agreed.

TRAVEL TIP

Getting There

Kunming Changshui International Airport connects to all major Chinese cities. Take Metro Line 18 directly to Stone Forest. Arrive before 9am to beat the crowds -- or go after 3pm when the tour groups leave.

大理

DALI -- BETWEEN MOUNTAIN AND WATER

The Three Pagodas have stood since the 9th century, white fingers pointing at a sky that has not changed in twelve centuries. Behind them, the 4,000-metre wall of Cangshan catches clouds like cotton. Below, Erhai Lake stretches 250 square kilometres of mountain-ringed water. The Bai people still farm these shores, still stretch goat's milk into translucent sheets of rushan. Dali was the seat of independent kingdoms that held the Tang Dynasty at bay for five centuries.

SIDE NOTE

Erhai Lake by Bike

Rent an electric scooter and ride the 130km lake loop. Stop in Xizhou for the best Bai architecture. In Zhoucheng, watch women dip cotton into indigo vats. Budget a full day -- you will stop constantly.

丽江

LIJIANG -- THE NAXI LABYRINTH

A town built without walls. The Naxi rulers said walls would box in their name -- the character for their surname inside a box means "trapped." So the old town sprawls open, a lattice of canals fed from Black Dragon Pool, dark wooden shophouses, and lantern-lit bridges. The Naxi developed Dongba -- the world's only living pictographic writing system. In the evening, ancient orchestras play Tang-dynasty compositions that have vanished everywhere else in China.

LOCAL SECRET

Skip the Crowds

Lijiang Old Town is packed after 10am. Arrive at dawn when the water wheels creak in silence and canals are empty. Or head to Shuhe Ancient Town, 3km north -- same Naxi charm, a fraction of the visitors, cheaper food.

玉龙雪山

THE JADE DRAGON

Thirteen jagged peaks strung together like a silver dragon soaring above the rooftops. The highest, Shanzidou, reaches 5,596 metres and has never been summited -- the Naxi consider it sacred. A cable car takes you to 4,506 metres where the air is thin and the silence is total. Below the snowline, Blue Moon Valley holds lakes so impossibly turquoise they look digitally enhanced. They are not.

HEALTH NOTE

Altitude Awareness

Kunming: 1,890m. Lijiang: 2,400m. Glacier viewpoint: 4,506m. Take it slow, drink water, skip alcohol on arrival day. Oxygen canisters available at the cable car station. Most people adjust fine within 24 hours.

虎跳峡

TIGER LEAPING GORGE

If you like your hikes dramatic and a little chaotic, this is the one. The Jinsha River -- the upper Yangtze -- has carved one of the deepest gorges on Earth between Jade Dragon and Haba Snow Mountains. The two-day high trail clings to the mountainside at 2,600 metres. In the morning, the valley fills with mist and you can hear the river roaring a vertical mile below. You walk through remote Naxi villages, past goat herds and waterfalls that cascade directly across the path. The "28 Bends" will test your legs. The view will repay every step.

PACKING LIST

Gorge Essentials

Good hiking shoes (the trail is rocky). Rain jacket. Headlamp. Snacks and 2L water. Start at Qiaotou, end at Tina's Guesthouse. Best months: April-June, Sep-Nov. Avoid July-Aug monsoon -- landslides close trail sections.

香格里拉

SHANGRI-LA -- THE LITTLE POTALA

The road climbs. The air thins. Prayer flags snap in wind that carries juniper and yak butter. At 3,160 metres, you have crossed an invisible border into the Tibetan Plateau. Songzanlin Monastery -- founded in 1679 by the Fifth Dalai Lama -- houses 700 monks. Red flying eaves, pink-white walls, and golden temple roofs shine under the intense plateau sunlight. Inside, butter lamps flicker in dark chapels and thangka paintings line every wall. Walking up the 146 steps at this altitude is a tiring exercise. Worth every breath.

FOOD TIP

Yak Everything

Yak butter tea (salty, buttery, addictive by the third cup). Yak meat hotpot at every restaurant. Tsampa (roasted barley flour) rolled by hand with butter tea. Spin the world's largest prayer wheel at Guishan Temple before dinner.

元阳

YUANYANG -- THE HANI TERRACES

The Hani people have been reshaping these mountains for sixty generations. Thousands of curved, flooded paddies cascade from 2,000m to 600m, following contours shaped entirely by hand. When the sun rises at Duoyishu, the changing colours of the water surface are dizzying -- villages loom through morning mist, and the Hani call this place "paradise on earth." Looking from the viewpoint, this landscape transports you back to when ancestors carved the mountain with bare hands and stone tools. UNESCO inscribed them in 2013. National Geographic called them the sexiest curves on Earth.

TIMING TIP

Sunrise Protocol

Duoyishu for sunrise (arrive before 6am). Bada for sunset and panoramas. Laohuzui for the most dramatic single composition. Best season: Nov-April when terraces are flooded. Hire a local driver (~300 RMB/day) -- the mountain roads are no joke.

FOOD GUIDE

MUST-EAT YUNNAN

Cross-Bridge Noodles -- Kunming icon. Boiling broth sealed under oil, you add the ingredients.

Steam Pot Chicken -- Jianshui clay pot, no water added. Pure distilled essence.

Wild Mushroom Hotpot -- 8-12 varieties. Seasonal June-Oct. The gov issues safety warnings.

Rushan -- Bai goat cheese fans, fried, drizzled with rose syrup. Only in Dali.

Erkuai -- Pounded rice cakes, grilled over charcoal with chili paste.

Pu'er Tea -- Pressed into cakes, aged for decades, traded like currency.

PRACTICAL

KNOW BEFORE YOU GO

Best time -- Mar-May or Sep-Nov. Avoid summer monsoon.

Getting around -- High-speed rail: KM to Dali ~2hrs, KM to LJ ~3hrs.

Payment -- WeChat Pay and Alipay everywhere. Carry some cash for villages.

Layers -- Weather shifts with altitude. Pack for 5C and 25C on the same day.

Language -- Mandarin basics help enormously. English is rare outside hotels.
