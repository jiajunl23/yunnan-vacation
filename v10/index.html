Plan a trip to Yunnan

Yunnan is one of China's most extraordinary provinces -- a place where snow-capped mountains, subtropical forests, ancient towns, and over 25 ethnic minority cultures coexist. It sits in the far southwest, bordering Myanmar, Laos, and Vietnam.

Start in Kunming, the capital. Known as the "Spring City" for its mild year-round climate (15-25C most days), it's the natural gateway for exploring the province.

  • Green Lake Park -- a central park where locals gather for tai chi and birdwatching. In winter, black-headed gulls migrate here by the thousands.
  • Yunnan Provincial Museum -- excellent collection covering the Bronze Age Dian Kingdom, ethnic minority textiles, and regional history.
  • Kunming Old Streets -- wander near Nanping Jie for local snacks, flower markets, and street life.

Just 85km southeast of Kunming lies the Stone Forest (Shilin), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Towering limestone pillars -- some over 30 meters tall -- formed over 270 million years when this area was an ancient seabed. The karst formations have eroded into blade-like pinnacles, archways, and labyrinthine corridors. The area is the homeland of the Sani people, a branch of the Yi ethnic group.

Plan 1-2 days in Kunming, plus a half-day for the Stone Forest.

What about Dali?

Dali is about 2 hours from Kunming by high-speed train. The old town sits between the Cangshan Mountains (nineteen peaks, up to 4,122m) and Erhai Lake, a pristine highland lake that shimmers different shades of blue throughout the day.

Dali is the heartland of the Bai people, one of Yunnan's largest ethnic minorities. Their culture permeates everything -- the architecture (white walls with ink-wash paintings), the food, the festivals.

  • Three Pagodas (Chongsheng Temple) -- Dali's iconic landmark, dating back to the 9th century. The tallest pagoda stands 69 meters. The reflection pool creates a perfect mirror image.
  • Dali Old Town -- stone streets lined with Bai-style buildings, tie-dye shops (zharan, a Bai specialty), and local cheese (rushan and rubing).
  • Erhai Lake -- rent a bike or e-scooter and ride the lakeside road. Stop at Bai fishing villages like Shuanglang and Xizhou.
  • Cangshan Mountains -- cable car up for panoramic views, or hike the Cloud Tourist Trail along the mountainside.
  • Xizhou Morning Market -- an authentic local market. Try a Xizhou baba (savory flatbread) fresh off the griddle.

2-3 days recommended. Dali has a wonderful relaxed energy.

Tell me about Lijiang

Lijiang is probably Yunnan's most famous destination. The Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site -- a maze of cobblestone lanes, arched bridges, and flowing canals dating back over 800 years to the Song Dynasty.

This is Naxi territory. The Naxi people have their own pictographic writing system called Dongba script -- one of the last living pictographic languages in the world.

  • Lijiang Old Town -- get deliberately lost in the winding alleys. Visit Mu Palace, climb to Wangu Tower on Lion Hill for panoramic views, and explore the quieter Shuhe Ancient Town nearby.
  • Jade Dragon Snow Mountain -- the massive 5,596m peak north of town. Cable car to 4,506m at Glacier Park. Blue Moon Valley at the base has surreal turquoise glacier-fed pools.
  • Black Dragon Pool -- the classic postcard view: a pavilion reflected in still water with Jade Dragon Snow Mountain behind it.
  • Baisha Village -- a quieter Naxi village about 8km from town. See the famous Baisha Murals (15th century) blending Buddhist, Taoist, and Dongba imagery.
  • Naxi Ancient Music -- evening concerts of traditional orchestral music passed down for centuries.

Budget 2-3 days. Early mornings and late evenings are the best times to feel its magic.

Best hiking?

Tiger Leaping Gorge is one of the deepest gorges in the world and arguably the best multi-day trek in all of China.

Located between Lijiang and Shangri-La, the Jinsha River (upper Yangtze) carves between Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (5,596m) and Haba Snow Mountain (5,396m). The gorge drops nearly 3,900 meters from mountaintops to river -- deeper than the Grand Canyon.

The classic High Trail hike:

  • Distance: roughly 22km along the upper path
  • Duration: 2 days / 1 night is standard, though some do it in one long day
  • Terrain: mountain trail with serious elevation changes. The "28 Bends" section is a grueling zigzag climb, but the views from the top are worth every step.
  • Accommodation: guesthouses along the trail -- Naxi Family Guesthouse and Tea Horse Guesthouse are popular stops with valley views, hot meals, and cold beer.
  • Best time: March to June and September to November. Avoid July-August (rainy, landslide risk).

Bring good shoes and sun protection. The scale of the mountains on either side is genuinely humbling.

Shangri-La?

Shangri-La (formerly Zhongdian) sits at 3,200 meters on the edge of the Tibetan Plateau. Renamed after the mythical paradise in James Hilton's "Lost Horizon," it's where Yunnan starts to feel like Tibet.

The air is thinner, the landscape vast -- rolling grasslands, yak herds, prayer flags, and monasteries against a backdrop of snow peaks. The population is predominantly Tibetan.

  • Songzanlin Monastery -- the largest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in Yunnan, often called the "Little Potala Palace." It houses over 600 monks. Golden roofs, massive prayer halls, and a sacred lake in front.
  • Dukezong Old Town -- one of the best-preserved Tibetan old towns. Features the world's largest prayer wheel (you need several people to turn it). Cozy Tibetan teahouses and handicraft shops.
  • Potatso (Pudacuo) National Park -- pristine alpine lakes, old-growth forests, and meadows. Bita Lake and Shudu Lake are highlights.
  • Napa Lake -- a seasonal wetland where Tibetan herders graze horses and yaks. In winter, endangered black-necked cranes migrate here.

Important: take it easy the first day, drink plenty of water, and avoid strenuous activity until you acclimate. About 4-5 hours by bus from Lijiang.

One more place

Yuanyang Rice Terraces -- in the far south of Yunnan, one of those places that genuinely looks like another planet.

The Hani people have been carving these terraces into the hillsides for over 1,300 years. Thousands of curved, flooded paddies cascade down steep mountainsides from 2,000m elevation to the valley floor. The entire system is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Bada Viewpoint -- the most famous vista. Over 3,000 terraces sweep down the mountain. At sunrise and sunset, the flooded terraces become mirrors reflecting every color in the sky.
  • Duoyishu -- the classic sunrise spot. Thousands of terraces catch the first light, creating a mosaic of gold, silver, and rose.
  • Laohuzui (Tiger Mouth) -- terraces curving in concentric patterns resembling a tiger's jaw. Spectacular at sunset.
  • Hani Villages -- mushroom-shaped thatched-roof houses clustered on ridgelines. The Hani people are welcoming, and the villages feel untouched by mass tourism.

Best time: November to March, when the terraces are flooded and become reflective pools. It's remote -- about 6-7 hours by bus from Kunming -- but that remoteness is part of the magic.

Food recommendations

Yunnan's food is unlike anything else in China -- closer to Southeast Asian flavors in many ways, with fresh herbs, wild mushrooms, sour and spicy profiles, and ingredients you won't find anywhere else.

  • Crossing-the-Bridge Rice Noodles (guoqiao mixian) -- Yunnan's most famous dish. A huge bowl of boiling broth arrives with platters of raw meat, vegetables, and rice noodles. You add everything yourself; the broth cooks it instantly.
  • Steam Pot Chicken (qiguo ji) -- chicken slow-cooked in a special clay pot with a central steam chimney. The broth that forms is incredibly pure and concentrated.
  • Wild Mushroom Hotpot -- Yunnan has more edible mushroom species than anywhere in China. In season (June-October), restaurants serve hotpots loaded with matsutake, porcini, chanterelles, and dozens of varieties you've never heard of.
  • Rushan and Rubing -- Bai-style dairy products. Rushan is fan-shaped dried milk cheese, often grilled and drizzled with rose jam. Rubing is pan-fried goat cheese. Both unique to Yunnan.
  • Erkuai -- chewy rice cakes, grilled or stir-fried, often served with chili sauce and pickled vegetables. A Kunming breakfast staple.
  • Yunnan Ham (Xuanwei huotui) -- dry-cured for years, similar to jamon iberico. Sliced thin as an appetizer or used to flavor soups.
  • Grilled Fish (Dali-style) -- whole fish from Erhai Lake, grilled over charcoal with Bai-style spiced sauce.
  • Tibetan Yak Butter Tea -- in Shangri-La, the local essential. Salty, rich, and warming at altitude.

For drinks: Yunnan is China's premier coffee-growing region (Pu'er area), and the specialty coffee scene is booming. Don't miss Pu'er tea -- the fermented dark tea traded along the Ancient Tea Horse Road for centuries. Visit a tea house for a proper gongfu tasting.

Practical tips

When to go:

  • March-May -- spring flowers, comfortable temps, fewer crowds. Best all-around season.
  • September-November -- clear skies, autumn colors, rice harvest. Excellent for photography.
  • June-August -- rainy season. Mushroom season though, and lush green landscapes.
  • December-February -- cold at altitude (Shangri-La can hit -10C), but Yuanyang terraces are at their best. Kunming stays mild.

Getting around:

  • High-speed trains connect Kunming to Dali (2 hours) and Lijiang (3 hours). Book on 12306.cn or Trip.com.
  • Buses fill the gaps -- Lijiang to Shangri-La (4-5 hours), Kunming to Yuanyang (6-7 hours).
  • Flights -- Kunming has a major international airport. Lijiang and Shangri-La have small airports.
  • E-scooter rentals are popular around Erhai Lake. Bring your passport.

Altitude: Lijiang sits at 2,400m, Shangri-La at 3,200m. Pack layers for 15-20C temperature swings in a single day. Strong sunscreen is essential -- UV is intense at elevation. Consider altitude sickness medication for Shangri-La (consult your doctor).

Money and connectivity: WeChat Pay and Alipay dominate, but carry some cash for rural areas. Set up a VPN before arrival for access to Google, WhatsApp, etc. English is limited outside tourist hubs -- download offline Chinese on a translation app.

Suggested itinerary: Kunming (1-2 days) - Dali (2-3 days) - Lijiang (2-3 days) - Tiger Leaping Gorge (2 days) - Shangri-La (2-3 days). Add Yuanyang as a separate trip from Kunming (2-3 days). Total: 12-17 days for the full circuit.
