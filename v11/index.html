QUEST #1: THE STONE LABYRINTH
DIFFICULTY: 2/5 ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠
QUEST STATUS: AVAILABLE

Navigate the ancient karst formations of Shilin, a geological dungeon 270 million years in the making. The limestone pillars rise like petrified sentinels, forming corridors and chambers that have confounded explorers since the age of the Sani people. Locals speak of a maze with no true center -- only those with patience will uncover its secrets.

Objectives

  • Traverse the Major Stone Forest without consulting the map
  • Locate the Sword Peak Pond hidden clearing
  • Photograph the Ashima Stone from the eastern approach
  • Complete the Minor Stone Forest loop trail

QUEST #2: THE THREE TOWERS OF DALI
DIFFICULTY: 1/5 ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠
QUEST STATUS: AVAILABLE

Three ancient towers stand sentinel at the foot of the Cang Mountains, relics of the Nanzhao Kingdom that once rivaled the Tang Dynasty. The central Qianxun Pagoda rises 69.13 meters into the Dali sky. Legends say the towers were built to suppress the dragons of Erhai Lake. Approach with reverence -- these are not mere ruins, but wards against chaos.

Objectives

  • Study the reflection of all three pagodas in the ornamental pool
  • Explore the Chongsheng Temple complex behind the towers
  • Identify the architectural differences between the central and flanking pagodas
  • Reach the upper terrace for the Cangshan panoramic view
REWARD: +800 XP // Kingdom Historian Badge // Nanzhao Lore Fragment

QUEST #3: THE MIRROR SEA
DIFFICULTY: 2/5 ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠
QUEST STATUS: AVAILABLE

The Bai people call it the Ear-Shaped Sea. From above, Erhai stretches 42 kilometers across the Dali basin, its still waters duplicating the snow-capped Cangshan range in perfect symmetry. Cycle its perimeter, charter a fishing vessel, or simply sit at its edge and let the altitude silence settle in. This is a zone for restoration, not combat.

Objectives

  • Cycle at least 30km of the lakeside ring road
  • Visit a Bai fishing village along the eastern shore
  • Witness sunrise over the lake from Caicun
  • Sample fresh lake fish at a waterfront stall
REWARD: +1,000 XP // Lakeside Wanderer Title // HP fully restored

QUEST #4: THE ANCIENT TRADE HUB
DIFFICULTY: 2/5 ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠
QUEST STATUS: AVAILABLE

A UNESCO-protected maze of cobblestone alleys and wooden shopfronts, Lijiang Old Town was the commercial crossroads of the Tea Horse Road. The Naxi people built their capital here without city walls -- an act of defiance or confidence, depending on who you ask. Navigate the canal-threaded streets at dawn before the merchant crowds spawn.

Objectives

  • Reach Sifang Square before 07:00 to avoid crowd debuffs
  • Follow the canal system from Mu Palace to the northern gate
  • Climb Lion Hill for the rooftop panorama
  • Locate a Dongba script inscription and record its meaning

QUEST #5: THE DRAGON'S SPINE
DIFFICULTY: 4/5 ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠
QUEST STATUS: AVAILABLE

A dual-objective raid quest. First, ascend the Jade Dragon Snow Mountain -- 5,596 meters of glaciated limestone that the Naxi revere as a god. The cable car delivers you to 4,506m, but the thin air hits like a debuff you cannot dispel. Then descend into Tiger Leaping Gorge, where the Jinsha River carves a 3,900-meter deep chasm between the Jade Dragon and Haba Snow Mountains. Legend says a tiger once leapt across the narrowest point. Your legs will wish they could do the same.

Objectives

  • Survive the cable car ascent to Glacier Park at 4,506m
  • Reach the observation platform without succumbing to altitude sickness
  • Hike the upper trail of Tiger Leaping Gorge (minimum 10km)
  • Locate the tiger-leap rock at the gorge narrows
  • Complete the 28 Bends switchback descent
REWARD: +2,500 XP // Dragonslayer Title // Altitude Resistance Passive Buff (+15%)

ELEVATION4,506m (peak access) / 2,670m (gorge trail)
DIFFICULTYHARD

QUEST #6: THE HIDDEN KINGDOM
DIFFICULTY: 3/5 ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠
QUEST STATUS: AVAILABLE

The name itself is a legend -- borrowed from James Hilton's fictional paradise, adopted by a real Tibetan plateau town at 3,160 meters. Songzanlin Monastery looms on the hillside like a fortress-temple hybrid, its 300-year-old halls filled with butter lamp light and chanted sutras. Below, the rebuilt Dukezong Old Town houses the world's largest prayer wheel. The air is thin, the yak butter tea is thick, and the sky is closer than you have ever seen it.

Objectives

  • Ascend to Songzanlin Monastery and enter the main prayer hall
  • Spin the giant prayer wheel in Dukezong Old Town
  • Drink yak butter tea with a local family
  • Walk the Napa Lake meadow loop at golden hour
  • Acclimatize for 24 hours before attempting strenuous activity
REWARD: +1,900 XP // Pilgrim of the Plateau Title // Inner Peace Buff (MP regen +20%)

BONUS QUEST: THE STAIRWAY FIELDS
DIFFICULTY: 3/5 ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠ ☠
QUEST STATUS: AVAILABLE

A hidden zone off the main quest line. The Hani people carved these rice terraces into the mountainside over 1,300 years, sculpting the landscape into thousands of shimmering mirrors during the flooded winter season. At sunrise, the terraces ignite in gradients of gold and silver. This is terrain modification on a civilizational scale -- an achievement no single player could unlock alone.

Objectives

  • Arrive at Bada viewpoint before sunrise
  • Descend into the terraces and walk the irrigation channels
  • Visit a Hani mushroom house village
  • Photograph the Duoyishu terraces at sunset

-- PRACTICAL INFORMATION --

Travel Configuration

FAST_TRAVEL_MODE:
Domestic flights to Kunming (KMG), Lijiang (LJG), or Shangri-La (DIG). Internal transfers via high-speed rail (Kunming-Dali: 2hr, Dali-Lijiang: 1.5hr) or bus.
RECOMMENDED_SEASON:
March through May (spring) or September through November (autumn). Avoid July-August monsoon debuff. Yuanyang terraces best in December-March when flooded.
CURRENCY:
CNY (Chinese Yuan). WeChat Pay and Alipay accepted nearly everywhere. Cash backup recommended for remote zones.
ALTITUDE_WARNING:
Shangri-La (3,160m) and Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (4,506m) require acclimatization. Carry Rhodiola or Hongjingtian tablets. Ascend gradually.
LANGUAGE_PACK:
Mandarin Chinese primary. Naxi, Bai, Tibetan, and Hani spoken in respective regions. English coverage: LOW outside tourist hubs. Download offline translation.
SAVE_FREQUENCY:
Photograph all tickets and bookings. Keep digital and paper copies of passport. Register at local PSB if staying at non-hotel accommodation.
DIFFICULTY_SCALING:
Budget: 150-250 CNY/day. Mid-range: 400-800 CNY/day. Premium: 1,000+ CNY/day. Hostels available in all major towns.
