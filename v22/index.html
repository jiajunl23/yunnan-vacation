A JOURNEY THROUGH

YUNNAN

Where the Himalayas descend into the tropics, and twenty-five peoples share one province of infinite variety

云南行记

Cinematic Scroll Edition

Our journey begins in the south, in the city that never needs a season name. Kunming sits at 1,900 metres on the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau, blessed with eternal spring. But it is what lies 85 kilometres to the southeast that stops every traveller short.

@YunnanExplorer Yunnan Guide

KUNMING

The Spring City. 15 degrees year-round. The Stone Forest rises like a geological fever dream 85km southeast -- 270 million years of limestone pinnacles sculpted by rain and chemical patience. The Sani people call it home. UNESCO called it irreplaceable.

2.3k #Yunnan #SpringCity #StoneForest

CHAPTER

TWO

大理

Northwest of Kunming, the road climbs into a landscape that has enchanted travellers for a thousand years. Dali sits on the western shore of Erhai Lake, beneath the long blue wall of the Cangshan mountains. For five centuries, this was the seat of independent kingdoms that held the Tang and Song dynasties at bay.

@DaliDreaming Yunnan Guide

DALI

The ancient kingdom of the Bai. Three Pagodas standing since the 9th century. Erhai Lake at dawn is a mirror laid flat against the sky. The Bai three-course tea ceremony -- first bitter, then sweet, then lingering -- is a philosophy in miniature.

5.1k #Dali #ErhaiLake #ThreePagodas

CHAPTER

THREE

丽江

@NaxiChronicle Yunnan Guide

LIJIANG

The Naxi kingdom beneath the Jade Dragon. A town built without walls -- an architectural statement of openness. Dongba script: the last living pictographic writing system on Earth. At dawn, before the crowds, the old town is a labyrinth of running water.

4.7k #Lijiang #Naxi #DongbaScript

The Jade Dragon rises to 5,596 metres above Lijiang. It has never been summited. Some mountains, the Naxi say, are not meant to be climbed. The southernmost glacier in the Northern Hemisphere clings to its peak -- a frozen sentinel watching over the old town below.

CHAPTER

FOUR

虎跳峡

@GorgeTrekker Yunnan Guide

TIGER LEAPING GORGE

THE HIKE THAT RUINS ALL OTHERS

3,900 metres of vertical drop. The Jinsha River -- the upper Yangtze -- has carved one of the deepest gorges on Earth. The two-day high trail clings to the mountainside at 2,600 metres, with the river a vertical mile below. Naxi guesthouses. Cold beer. Hot meals. Earned.

8.3k #TigerLeapingGorge #TheHike

CHAPTER

FIVE

香格里拉

The road climbs. The air thins. Prayer flags appear on the passes, snapping in wind that carries the scent of juniper and yak butter. At 3,200 metres, you have crossed an invisible border. This is no longer China as most of the world imagines it. This is the Tibetan Plateau.

@PlateauPilgrim Yunnan Guide

SHANGRI-LA

Altitude: 3,160m. Songzanlin Monastery -- the Little Potala -- rises above the town like a fortress of faith. 300 monks. Founded 1679 by the Fifth Dalai Lama. Butter tea served to all visitors. In the evening, locals dance in slow communal circles. Not a performance. Just life.

6.2k #ShangriLa #Songzanlin #TibetanPlateau

CHAPTER

SIX

元阳

@TerracePilgrim Yunnan Guide

YUANYANG

1,300 YEARS OF PATIENCE

The Hani people have been reshaping these mountains for sixty generations. Thousands of curved, flooded paddies cascading from 2,000m to 600m. At sunrise they turn to hammered gold. At sunset, sheets of copper and rose. UNESCO World Heritage since 2013.

12.5k #Yuanyang #HaniTerraces #UNESCO
@YunnanFlavours Yunnan Guide

THE FOOD OF YUNNAN

A PROVINCE THAT EATS WHAT THE MOUNTAINS PROVIDE

  • Cross-Bridge Noodles -- broth so hot it seals beneath a layer of oil
  • Steam Pot Chicken -- no water added, pure distilled essence of bird
  • Wild Mushrooms -- more varieties than any region on Earth
  • Rushan -- Bai milk fans, fried and drizzled with rose syrup
  • Pu'er Tea -- pressed into cakes, aged for decades, traded like currency
  • Erkuai -- pounded rice cakes, grilled, slathered with chili paste
18.9k #YunnanFood #Mushrooms #Puer
