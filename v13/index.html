Kunming - The Spring City!!

this is the Stone Forest and it is SO COOL

Kunming is the first place we went to. It is called the Spring City because it is always spring there! That is so cool! The weather was really nice and not too hot and not too cold.

We went to the Stone Forest and the rocks were SO BIG. They are 270 million years old. That is even older than dinosors dinosaurs!! Some of the rocks looked like animals and some looked like people. I saw one that looked like a big elephant.

There were also really pretty flowers everywhere. Kunming has lots and lots of flowers. My mom said they grow flowers there and send them all over China!

My favorite thing: The Stone Forest because the rocks were like a giant maze and I pretended I was an explorer discovering a secret world!
I learned that... the Stone Forest rocks were made by water a really really long time ago. The water wore away the soft parts and left the hard parts standing up. That is called erosion!
I give it: ★★★★★
(my drawing of me standing next to a really tall rock)
2

Dali - The Three Pagodas!

these are the Three Pagodas and they are super old

Next we went to Dali! Dali is a really old city with a big wall around it. The coolest thing there is the Three Pagodas. A pagoda is like a really tall tower. There are three of them and they are over 1000 years old!!

The biggest one is 69 meters tall. That is like a 20 story building!! My dad said people built them without any mashines machines. They just used their hands and simple tools. That is amazing.

There is also a really pretty pool of water in front of them and you can see the pagodas reflected in the water. I took a picture of it for my project!

My favorite thing: Seeing the reflection of the pagodas in the water. It looked like there were six pagodas instead of three!
I learned that... the Three Pagodas survived a really big earthquake a long time ago. They are super strong even though they are so old!
I give it: ★★★★
SO COOL!
3

Erhai Lake!

Erhai Lake is really big and really blue

Erhai Lake is right next to Dali and it is SO pretty. The water is really blue and you can see big mountains behind it. The montains mountains are called Cangshan and they are really tall!

We rode bikes along the lake and it took a really long time because the lake is really big. My dad said it is the second biggest lake in Yunnan. Erhai means "ear-shaped sea" because it looks like an ear from above! That is funny.

We also went on a boat and I got to see fishermen catching fish. They had these cool birds called cormorants that catch fish for them!

My favorite thing: Riding bikes next to the lake because the wind was blowing and the mountains were so pretty and I felt really free!
I learned that... the Bai people live around Erhai Lake. They have their own language and their own special clothes and food. They are one of China's 56 ethnic groups!
I give it: ★★★★★
(my drawing of the lake with mountains and a little boat)
4

Lijiang Old Town!

all the buildings in Lijiang are really really old

Lijiang Old Town is one of the coolest places I have ever been!! All the buildings are really old and there are little streams of water running through the streets. It is like a fairy tale town!

The town is over 800 years old! The Naxi people built it. They have their own special writing that looks like little pictures. My mom bought me a necklace with Naxi writing on it. It is beautful beautiful.

At night there were lots of red lanterns and it was so pretty. We got to eat snacks and walk around and listen to music. I never wanted to leave!

My favorite thing: Walking around at night with all the lanterns. It felt like being in a movie!
I learned that... the Naxi people have a special writing called Dongba script. It is one of the only picture-writing systems still used in the world! Each little picture means a word.
I give it: ★★★★★
WOW! SO COOL!
5

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain!!

the mountain has SNOW on it even in summer!!

This mountain is SO AMAZING. It is called Jade Dragon Snow Mountain because it looks like a dragon lying down with snow on it! The highest part is 5,596 meters tall. That is really really high!!

We took a cable car up the mountain and it was kind of scary but also really fun. When we got to the top it was SO COLD and there was snow everywhere! My ears hurt because the air is really thin up high. My dad said it is because there is less oxigen oxygen up there.

The view from the top was the best thing I have ever seen. You can see so far! Everything looks tiny from up there.

My favorite thing: Touching the snow at the top! I made a tiny snowball and my hands got really cold but it was worth it.
I learned that... nobody has ever climbed to the very top of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain! It is too dangerous. The Naxi people think it is a sacred mountain.
I give it: ★★★★★
(my drawing of the mountain with snow and a dragon shape)
6

Tiger Leaping Gorge!

the water goes SO FAST through the gorge

Tiger Leaping Gorge is one of the deepest gorges gorges in the WHOLE WORLD. A gorge is like a really deep and narrow valley with a river at the bottom. The river is the Jinsha River and it goes SO fast!

It is called Tiger Leaping Gorge because there is a story that a tiger jumped across the river at the narrowest part! The tiger used a big rock in the middle to jump. I don't know if that is true but it is a cool story.

The walls of the gorge are 3,790 meters deep. That is like stacking 10 of the tallest buildings in the world on top of each other!! We walked on a trail along the edge and I held my mom's hand because it was kind of scary.

My favorite thing: Looking down at the river from the trail. The water was white and foamy and SO loud!
I learned that... the Jinsha River is part of the Yangtze River, which is the longest river in Asia! The water in Tiger Leaping Gorge will eventually flow all the way to the ocean.
I give it: ★★★★★
WOW!
7

Shangri-La and the Big Monastery!

this is the Songzanlin Monastery and it is HUGE

Shangri-La is the last big city we visited and it is way up in the mountains. It was really cold there! The Songzanlin Monastery is the biggest Tibetan Buddhist monastry monastery in Yunnan. It looks like a golden palace!

There were monks in red robes walking around and they were really nice. The buildings had gold roofs that were super shiny in the sun. Inside there were big statues and colorful paintings on the walls.

The air is really thin in Shangri-La because it is 3,160 meters above sea level. I got a little dizzy but it was okay. My dad said it is called altitude sickness.

My favorite thing: The golden roofs of the monastery shining in the sun. They were so bright and pretty!
I learned that... Songzanlin Monastery was built in 1679. That is over 300 years ago! It is sometimes called the "Little Potala Palace" because it looks like the big famous one in Tibet.
I give it: ★★★★
(my drawing of the monastery with gold roofs and prayer flags)
8

Yuanyang Rice Terraces!

the rice terraces look like stairs for a giant

The Yuanyang Rice Terraces are SO COOL. They look like big stairs going up the mountain! The Hani people made them over 1,300 years ago so they could grow rice on the mountains. That is really smart!

When the sun rises the water in the terraces turns all gold and pink and purple. We woke up really early to see the sunrise and it was the most beatiful beautiful thing I have ever seen. My mom cried a little bit because it was so pretty.

The Hani people are really nice. They showed us how they plant rice. You have to stand in the water and push the little rice plants into the mud. It is really hard work!

My favorite thing: Watching the sunrise over the rice terraces. All the water turned golden and it looked like a million mirrors on the mountain!
I learned that... the Yuanyang Rice Terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. That means the whole world thinks they are special and important and we need to protect them!
I give it: ★★★★★
WOW! SO COOL!

Things to Pack!

If you go to Yunnan you should bring these things:

  • A warm jacket (it gets cold in the mountains!!)
  • Sunscreen (the sun is really strong up high)
  • Comfy walking shoes
  • A rain jacket (it rains a lot)
  • A camera to take pictures
  • A water bottle
  • Snacks for long car rides
  • A notebook to draw in
  • Medicine for altitude sickness
  • A good attitude!!

Tips from me:

  • Drink lots of water when you are up high in the mountains
  • Learn to say "hello" in the local languages -- the people love it!
  • Wake up early for sunrises -- they are the BEST
  • Try all the food even if it looks weird
  • Bring a good book for the long drives between cities
